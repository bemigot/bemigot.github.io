## Why local-first

Most learning tools today live in someone else's cloud. Your notes, your
progress, the model you talk to — all rented, all remote. For a project about
*self-learning*, that arrangement sits badly. Learning is personal and
long-lived; the tools that support it should be too.

*Local-first* means the learner's data lives on the learner's device first, and
syncs outward only when it helps. The app keeps working offline. Nothing
disappears when a service shuts down. And as small models get genuinely capable,
more of the loop — practice, feedback, spaced review — can run on hardware you
already own.

## What this means for Bemigot

The questions I want to explore here:

- How much of a language- or programming-tutor loop can run on-device today?
- Where is the cloud still genuinely necessary, and where is it just convenient?
- What does *ownership* of one's learning history actually buy a learner?
